About Me

Undergraduate Researcher!

I am a third-year B.Tech Engineering Physics undergraduate at IIT Patna (CGPA 8.34), specializing in optical and electro-optical instrumentation, imaging systems, and optical sensors & detectors for space, astronomical, and precision measurement applications. My research experience spans laser-based experimental optics, microscopy, near-field light confinement, and system-level optical simulations. At ISRO (SAC), I worked on beaconless acquisition, tracking, and pointing (ATP) simulations for optical satellite communication, modeling Gaussian pointing errors and optimizing scanning strategies under SWaP constraints. I actively seek research internships in Optical Sensors & Detectors, Imaging & Telescope Instrumentation, and Optical Calibration in national laboratories and international observatories.

Core Research

Single Beam Gradient Force Optical Trap

Supervisor: Dr. V. R. Dantham, IIT Patna

Duration: Jan 2025 – Ongoing

Designed and aligned a single-beam gradient-force optical trapping system using high-NA microscope optics. Achieved stable 3D trapping of PMMA microspheres (<2 μm) using a 650 nm diode laser. Experimentally generated and characterized photonic nanojets using dielectric microspheres for near-field optical confinement. Currently developing a CO₂ laser (10.6 μm) nanojet-assisted trapping system for enhanced trapping of metallic nanoparticles. Gained extensive hands-on expertise in optical alignment, beam shaping, microscope design, laser diagnostics, and laboratory safety.

Techniques: Optical Sensors, Imaging Optics, Optical Calibration, Light–Matter Interaction

Beaconless ATP

Supervisor: Dr. Pranav Pandey, Head- OCSD-QUTG/HSTA, Space Application Centre, ISRO

Duration: May 2025 – Aug 2025

Developed a Python-based simulation framework for beaconless optical Acquisition, Tracking, and Pointing (ATP) systems for satellite laser communication. Modeled Gaussian pointing errors and derived Rayleigh and Rician acquisition probability distributions. Implemented and compared spiral and raster scanning strategies for ground–satellite and inter-satellite links across LEO, MEO, and GEO scenarios.

Evaluated system performance under FSM bandwidth, detector response time, beam divergence, wavelength, laser power, and receiver aperture constraints, demonstrating SWaP-optimized optical terminal architectures aligned with ISRO’s optical communication roadmap.

Focus: Optical Signal Processing, Detectors, Imaging Systems, Space Optics
